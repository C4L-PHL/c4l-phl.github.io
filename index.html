Why Philly?

Philadelphia is well known for its important role in US history, but it is also a dynamic and modern city, with a vibrant art scene, incredible food, and great public transit, as well as hundreds of libraries, museums and universities. The New York Times recently ranked Philly as the #3 place in the world to visit, and we couldn't agree with them more. Plus Philly packs it all into a fairly small and compact Center City that is easy to get around, so all of it will be at your fingertips. So many of us are a part of the code4lib community — we're excited to take our turn in hosting the national code4lib conference and continue the tradition of making it as welcoming as possible.

Financial Sketch

Based on the Raleigh hosting team's experience, we plan to engage a conference planning organization to negotiate all of our contracts, including venue, hotel, wireless, catering, and AV. We have done our research and know that Philly has plenty of venues & hotels that can accommodate a conference of our size, but we know that letting a professional planner request the bid will keep prices as low as possible. We therefore have a projected budget based on previous years since we do not have exact quotes. We expect:

  • Registration fee between $200-250.
  • Hotel rates between $160-$180
  • Up to 500 attendees

We are committed to working hard towards strong fundraising goals to help keep the cost as low as possible for attendees.

Ideas: New and Mature

  • With so many universities and cultural institutions in Philadelphia, we are planning to hold the pre-conferences in distributed locations around the city. As far possible, we will cluster them close together to make getting between sessions easy. As well as enabling you to see more places around the city, this should also help keep costs low.
  • We feel the conference is ready for multi-track. We'd like to try it for a half-day. Specific tracks will be determined based on presentations selected.

Calendar

A target range of dates has been narrowed down to February through March. Exact dates will be determined by venue and hotel availability and price.

Transportation

Philadelphia has great links from all over the country.

  • The Philadelphia International Airport (PHL) is just 20 minutes from Center City either by train ($7), airport shuttle (~$15) or cab (~$25-$30).
  • Amtrak to Philly goes up and down the East coast with good connections from the Midwest.
  • Once you get here, Philly has great public transit including multiple train lines, trolleys and buses (SEPTA).
  • Philly is also rated as one of the best walking cities in the country, with an easy-to-understand grid street design and new "Walk! Philadelphia" street signage to help you navigate the city.

Restaurants

Philly's food scene has exploded in the past decade with so many great options. Soul food, tapas, BYO, farm-to-table, renowned chefs, vegetarian / vegan, and more. Some highlights include:

  • Barbuzzo - Mediterranean Kitchen
  • Vedge - Vegan Bliss
  • Sabrina's - Breakfasty Comfort food
  • Reading Terminal Market - Hundreds of choices under one roof

Philly also has great delis everywhere, awesome donuts, a handful of local coffee roasters, and of course, The Cheesesteak!

Tech

A small but lively Tech scene:

  • Duck Duck Go, search engine of choice for privacy-loving librarians
  • Multiple hacker/maker spaces (The Hactory, Hive76, NextFab plus more), and the possibility of a preconference at one of them.
  • Strong Open Civic Data & Civic hacking communities.
  • The largest and most active chapter of Girl Develop It.
  • N3rd St, our official tech corridor.

History

  • The Liberty Bell, a testament to Philadelphia's ability to make things that break on first use.
  • Independence Hall, birthplace of both the Declaration of Independence and the Constitution.
  • Carpenters Hall, meeting place of the first Continental Congress.
  • Edgar Allen Poe National Historic Site, the home where Poe was at his most productive.
  • Betsy Ross House, the site where the seamstress and flag-maker Betsy Ross, purportedly lived when she sewed the first American Flag.
  • National Constitution Center, housing a rare, original copy of the first public printing of the Constitution.
  • Valley Forge National Historic Park, site of the 1777-78 winter encampment of the Continental Army.

Outdoor Recreation

  • Multiple ice skating rinks, including: Rothman Rink at Dilworth Park, in front of the Amazing City Hall, and Blue Cross Riverrink, along the Philly waterfront.
  • Jogging along the Schuylkill into Fairmount Park, one of the world's largest park systems and home of the famous Boathouse Row.
  • Penn's Landing and the Delaware Waterfront, the recently revived waterfront area with year-round activities (yes, winter too).
