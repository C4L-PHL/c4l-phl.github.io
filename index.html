The City of Brotherly Love

Philadelphia is well known for its important role in US history, but it is also dynmic and modern city, with a vibrant art scene, incredible food, and great public transit, as well as hundreds of libraries, museums and universities. The New York Times recently ranked Philly as the #3 place in the world to visit, and we couldn't agree with them more. Plus Philly packs it all into a fairly small and compact Center City that is easy to walk around. So many of us are a part of the code4lib community — we're excited to take our turn in hosting the national code4lib conference!

Financial Sketch

Based on the Raleigh hosting team's experience, we plan to engage a conference planning organization to negotiate all of our contracts, including venue, hotel, wireless, catering, and AV. We therefore have a projected budget based on previous years, but we do not have exact quotes. We expect:

  • Registration fee between $200-250.
  • Hotel rates around $175
  • Up to 500 attendees

We are committed to strong fundraising goals and controlling costs to keep the cost per atendees as low as possible.

Ideas: New and Mature

  • Scavenger hunt-style preconferences! With so many universities and cultural institutions in Philadelphia proper, we would love to hold preconferences in different locations around the city.
  • We feel the conference is ready for multi-track. We'd like to try it for a half-day. Specific tracks will be determined based on presentations selected.

Calendar

A target range of dates has been narrowed down to February through March.

Transportation

Philadelphia has great links from all over the country.

  • The Philadelphia International Airport (PHL) is just 20 minutes from Center City either by train ($7), airport shuttle (~$15) or cab (~$25-$30).
  • Amtrak to Philly goes up and down the East cost with good connections from the midwest.
  • One you get here, Philly has great public transit including mutiple train lines, trolleys and buses

Restaurants

Philly's food scene has exploded in the past decade with so many great options. Soul food, tapas, BYO, farm-to-table, renowned chefs, vegetarian / vegan, and more. Some higlights include:

  • Barbuzzo - Mediterranen Kitchen
  • Vedge - Vegan Bliss
  • Sabrina's - Breakfasty Comfort food
  • Reading Terminal Market - Hundreds of choices under on roof

Philly also has great delis everywhere, awesome donuts, plus a handful of local coffee roasters and plenty of cafes that serve them.
